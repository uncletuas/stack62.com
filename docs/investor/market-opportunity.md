# Stack62 — Market Opportunity

## Global SMB Software Market

Small and mid-sized businesses (1–249 employees) represent:
- **99%** of all registered businesses globally
- **~$120B** annual spend on business software (Gartner, 2025)
- **$45B+** annual productivity loss from manual, spreadsheet-driven operations (IDC estimate)

Despite this, the vast majority of SMB software is either:
- Too generic (G-Suite, Notion) — misses operational depth
- Too expensive and complex (SAP, Salesforce) — requires implementation partners
- Too fragile (no-code tools) — breaks at scale, requires constant maintenance

**Stack62 addresses the gap** between "generic productivity tools" and "enterprise ERP" — a segment that is both massive and chronically underserved.

## Market Tailwinds

### 1. AI Normalisation in the Workplace
- 68% of SMB decision-makers report they would adopt AI tools if they could trust the output (Deloitte SMB Tech Survey, 2025)
- Stack62's **Plan → Diff → Approve** governance model directly addresses this trust barrier

### 2. No-Code Fatigue
- Airtable, Notion, and Monday.com have plateaued in SMB market share
- Businesses report "tool sprawl" and "customization walls" as primary reasons for churning from no-code platforms

### 3. Developer Scarcity
- Average time-to-hire for a backend engineer: 3–4 months globally
- Average cost: $80K–$150K/year salary
- Stack62 delivers an equivalent capability at 0.5% of that cost

### 4. Regulatory Pressure
- GDPR, CCPA, and sector-specific regulations increasingly require audit trails and data governance
- Stack62's built-in audit log and permission policies are compliance-ready out of the box

### 5. Multi-channel Business Communication
- WhatsApp is the primary business communication channel in Africa, MENA, and Southeast Asia
- Stack62's native WhatsApp integration unlocks markets where email-first tools struggle
