# SLIDE 3: THE SOLUTION

## Meet Stack62: The AI Coworker

Stack62 gives every business an AI agent that understands its workflows and builds the systems it needs — with human approval at every step.

### How It Works: Plan → Diff → Approve

```
Manager: "We need a procurement approval workflow with supplier tracking"
         ↓
Coworker: Understands context, designs the system
         ↓
Stack62: Shows a detailed diff: "I'm creating these 3 forms, 2 workflows, 
         and these permission rules"
         ↓
Manager: Reviews, suggests changes, approves
         ↓
System: Goes live immediately — fully operational
```

### The Core Moat: Systems Live on Stack62

Unlike tools that generate code and hand it back, Stack62 systems **persist and evolve on the platform**:

- ✅ Real data records, not just exports
- ✅ Continuous AI assistance (improve the system without rebuilding)
- ✅ Version history and rollback
- ✅ Audit trail of every change
- ✅ Built-in workflows, integrations, and governance

### Key Differentiator: Governance-First AI

**Every AI-generated change:**
- Is scored for risk
- Is shown as a detailed diff
- Requires explicit human approval before going live

This is the trust layer that enterprise buyers demand — and it's not optional.

# APPENDIX

## FAQ

### Q: How is Stack62 different from no-code tools like Airtable or Notion?

**A:** Stack62 systems run on the platform, not exported. This means:
- Continuous AI assistance (improve systems over time)
- Versioning and rollback
- Built-in approval workflows with audit trails
- Governance-first from day one
- Businesses stay on Stack62, creating lock-in

Airtable and Notion are generic productivity layers. Stack62 is an operating system for business workflows.

### Q: How do you prevent AI from making bad decisions?

**A:** Every AI-generated change goes through Plan → Diff → Approve:
1. **Plan:** Coworker designs the system
2. **Diff:** Shows exactly what's being created (forms, workflows, permissions)
3. **Approve:** Humans review and sign off before anything goes live

This is the trust layer that enterprises demand.

### Q: What about data privacy and security?

**A:** 
- **On-premise AI:** Use Ollama for data sovereignty (AI stays on your servers)
- **No training on customer data:** We don't use your data to train models
- **SOC 2 Type II roadmap:** Full audit trails, encryption at rest, regular backups
- **Role-based access control:** Granular permissions per org and per system
- **GDPR ready:** Data residency, right to deletion, audit logs

### Q: How do you handle vendor lock-in with AI models?

**A:** Stack62 supports multiple AI providers:
- **Anthropic (Claude)** — Production default
- **OpenRouter** — Access to multiple models
- **Ollama** — Self-hosted, on-premise

Customers can switch providers without rebuilding systems. AI context is model-agnostic.

This includes:
